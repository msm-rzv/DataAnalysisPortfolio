CUSTOMER SHOPPING BEHAVIOUR

JUNE 2025

"A data analysis of 3,900 retail customers examining spending patterns, customer loyalty, product performance, and the impact of subscriptions and discounts on revenue."

Problem Statement

"What are the key drivers of customer spending behaviour, and how can the business use purchase patterns, loyalty segmentation, and product performance to improve revenue and customer retention?"

DATA CLEANING

Before loading into PostgreSQL, the raw dataset was cleaned using Python. Six steps were applied to ensure the data was analysis-ready.

37 missing Review Rating
values imputed

Missing Value Imputation

Null Review Ratings were filled using the median rating within each product category — preserving category-level patterns rather than applying a single global median across all products.

100% match between
promo_code_used & discount_applied

Redundant Column Removed

The promo_code_used column was found to be 100% identical to discount_applied across all 3,900 rows. It was confirmed redundant and dropped before loading to PostgreSQL.

2 new features
engineered

Feature Engineering

Two new columns were created — age_group using quartile-based age bands (Young Adult, Adult, Middle-aged, Senior), and purchase_frequency_days mapping text frequency labels to numeric days for quantitative analysis.

KEY FINDINGS

Thirteen SQL queries across four sections uncovering patterns in revenue, products, loyalty, and spend behaviour.

67.7% revenue from male customers
vs 32.3% female

Revenue & Demographics

Male customers generate $157,890 vs $75,191 for female customers — reflecting their larger share of the dataset (68% vs 32%) rather than higher individual spend. Revenue is almost evenly distributed across customers aged 25 and above, while Young Adults (18–24) contribute significantly less.

$0.38 difference in avg spend
subscribers vs non-subscribers

Subscriptions Don't Drive Spend

Subscribed customers average $59.49 per transaction vs $59.87 for non-subscribers — a negligible difference of $0.38. Subscription status alone does not predict higher individual spend, suggesting the business needs a stronger value proposition for its subscription programme.

79.9% of customers are Loyal
only 2.1% are New

High Retention, Low Acquisition

3,116 of 3,900 customers (79.9%) have made more than 10 previous purchases — an exceptionally high loyalty rate. However only 83 customers (2.1%) are new, suggesting the business retains customers effectively but is struggling to attract new ones.

72.4% of repeat buyers
are not subscribed

The Subscription Gap

Even among customers with more than 5 previous purchases, 2,518 out of 3,476 (72.4%) are not subscribed. Loyal customers who have clearly committed to the brand are not being converted to subscribers — a targeted campaign for this group could significantly grow the subscriber base.

50.3% discount rate on Blouse
the top selling product

Discounts Follow Popularity

The five most discounted products are almost identical to the five most purchased products. Discounts are currently boosting already popular items rather than driving demand for underperformers like Jeans (124 orders, the least purchased product).

INSIGHTS

"Male customers account for 67.7% of revenue — reflecting customer base composition rather than higher individual spend."

"Subscribed and non-subscribed customers spend virtually the same per transaction — $59.49 vs $59.87."

"79.9% of customers are Loyal, but only 2.1% are New — strong retention, weak acquisition."

"72.4% of repeat buyers are not subscribed — the clearest conversion opportunity in the dataset."

"Gloves and Boots are among the least purchased products but have the highest satisfaction ratings."

"Discounts are concentrated on top sellers — not being used to boost underperforming products."

RECOMMENDATIONS

Four data-driven actions the business can take based on these findings.

2,518 loyal non-subscribers
to target

Launch a Subscription Conversion Campaign

With 2,518 repeat buyers who are not subscribed, even a 20% conversion rate would nearly double the subscriber base. These customers have already demonstrated loyalty — they need a compelling reason to subscribe, not further convincing to shop.

124 orders for Jeans
the weakest performer

Redirect Discount Strategy

Discounts are currently concentrated on already top-selling products. Applying promotions to underperforming items like Jeans (124 orders) or Backpacks (143 orders) could help balance the product portfolio without cannibalising top-line revenue.

2.1% of customers
are new

Invest in New Customer Acquisition

Only 83 out of 3,900 customers are new. The business retains customers exceptionally well but is not growing its base. Targeted campaigns aimed at the 18–24 demographic — which currently contributes the least revenue — could open a new acquisition channel.

3.86 avg rating for Gloves
least purchased top-rated item

Promote High-Rated Low-Volume Products

Gloves and Boots have the highest customer satisfaction scores in the dataset but sit among the least purchased products. Increased product visibility, bundling, or seasonal campaigns could convert strong satisfaction into stronger sales volume.

Closing Note "This business has an exceptionally loyal customer base but faces two structural challenges: low new customer acquisition and a large pool of loyal non-subscribers. Addressing these two gaps — through targeted acquisition and a subscription conversion campaign — represents the clearest path to sustainable revenue growth."
